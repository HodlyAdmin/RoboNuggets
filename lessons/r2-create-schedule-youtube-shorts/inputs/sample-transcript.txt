Speaker:
If you want to make better short-form content, stop trying to sound important and start trying to sound clear.

Most people bury their best line halfway through a paragraph because they are warming up on camera.
Lead with the sharpest sentence first.

Every great clip has one emotional turn.
It surprises the viewer, reframes a familiar idea, or says the quiet part out loud.

The goal is not to sound smarter.
The goal is to make the listener feel like they understood something valuable in less than thirty seconds.
